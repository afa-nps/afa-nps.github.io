Creating a Space of Their Own

Below is a Google Map of 200+ Historic African American Leisure Space

Throughout the late 19th century and early 20th century, African American continued to create leisure spaces for themselve. These spaces took the shape of beach, ranch, and mountain resorts. Many of these spaces were originally created for affluent African American. Yet, many of these Black elites saw it as their responsibility to uplift African Americans of low socioeconomics. Their paternalistic actions help create experiences in spaces for African American across all class lines, while simutaneouly excluding lower-class African Americans from areas elites designated only for themselves.

Overtime many of these places disappear. This happen due to a multitude of reasons. Afrer the start of the Civil Rights Movement and desegregation many of these places began to loose business. However, some properties were vandalized or destroyed by arson.
